# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

We want participation in this project to be safe, respectful, and welcoming,
regardless of experience, identity, background, or ability. Maintainers and
contributors are expected to help create a learning environment where people
can ask questions, make mistakes, and improve.

## Expected behavior

- Communicate professionally, patiently, and constructively.
- Give technical feedback about the work, not the person.
- Respect different experience levels and explain unfamiliar terms.
- Protect private information and follow responsible-security practices.
- Accept correction, acknowledge mistakes, and de-escalate conflict.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, discrimination, threats, insults, or sexualized conduct.
- Publishing another person's private information without permission.
- Deliberately unsafe scanning guidance, credential exposure, or abuse of the
  project against unauthorized systems.
- Sustained disruption, spam, or bad-faith participation.

## Reporting and enforcement

Open a repository issue for non-sensitive moderation concerns. For serious or
private conduct involving GitHub, use GitHub's
[Report Abuse form](https://support.github.com/contact/report-abuse). Do not put
private information in a public issue. Maintainers will review reports fairly,
protect privacy where practical, and may warn, temporarily restrict, or
permanently remove a participant depending on the impact and pattern of behavior.

This policy applies in project spaces and when someone officially represents
the project elsewhere. It is informed by the community standards in the
[Contributor Covenant](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/3/0/).
